Light-sheet imaging of retinal cell activity

Built on the foundation of restricted planar imaging, light-sheet fluorescence imaging allows measurements of neural activity at single-cell resolution across large swathes of ex-vivo retina (read more). We are refining these tools to study precise signal communication within compartments such as dendrites and axon terminals, and across synaptic layers, in genetically targeted populations of interneurons and retinal ganglion cells.

Image analysis

Image analysis is an essential component in studies ranging from functional assessment of retinal cells to early detection of retinal diseases. We are developing and incorporating new methods of image analysis into our studies, to reliably extract neural activity across large populations of retinal neurons and interpreting patterns of activity in the context of visual stimuli. Check out our 1-photon image analysis pipeline.

Visual stimuli

We are working on expanding the repertoire of visual stimuli from artificial to natural images for use in experiments. We welcome new and interesting ideas towards this effort.
